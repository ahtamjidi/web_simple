Ali-akbar Agha-mohammadi (Ali Agha)

 

I am a roboticist interested in stochastic control systems and estimation / filtering theory, with applications to the "joint" design of planning and perception for robotic systems. I am currently a Robotics Research Technologist at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), California Institute of Technology (Caltech).

Before joining NASA, I was a Research Staff Engineer (Roboticist) in Qualcomm Research (2015-2016) where I worked on Quadrotor Autonomy. Prior to that, I was a postdoctoral fellow at MIT (2013-2015) working with Jonathan P. How and I was a member of of LIDS (Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems) and ACL (Aerospace Controls Laboratory). I got my PhD in Computer Engineering from Texas A&M University in 2013.
